Pune is a straging city in the western Indian state of Maharashtra. It was once the prime establishment of Peshwas (prime ministers) of the Maratha Empire in the 18th century. Pune is known by many names and loved for its pleasing weather.
The city name Pune came from the word Punya, which means merging of the two rivers - Muthi and Mula Rivers.
There are more names according to the historical records.
It is the 9th most populous city with an approximate population of 7.4 million as of 2020 in Maharashtra, India.
It has been nominated as “the most liveable city of India”.
Pune is widely recognized as the country's second prime IT hub and an apex automobile and manufacturing hub.
Moreover, Pune is renowned as the Oxford East with an extensive range of educational institutions.
In recent decades, the city has become the emerging and fastest-growing educational hub with around half of the aggregate number of international students studying in Pune.
